About Me

My name is Leslie Lei, and I love learning about usability and accessibility to create easy-to-use websites and apps. I am deeply interested about self-growth and improvement, and I actively look for ways to improve.


I have a degree in Informatics or Information Science from the University of California, Irvine. As a student, I have learned about usability, accessibility, and design in my classes. As a team member and leader of project groups, I have learned about planning, communication, compassion, and flexibility. I strive to bring these qualities and knowledge to the projects that I work on.

My Values

Learning

I believe that there is always something to be learned. Whether it be playing a sport or designing software, I realized that there were many factors that contribute to success. Acquiring knowledge gets us closer to that success. That is why after finishing my formal education, I continue to learn from online classes and from attending hackathons.

Compassion

I value compassion because I know what it is like to have tough days. And in those cases, giving compassion to myself did not make me weak or lazy. It gave me the strength to continue on with what I needed to do. Compassion teaches oneself that you are deserving of it and that you are more than what you struggle with.

Communication

I value communication because I have learned from projects that I have worked on the importance of communication, responsiveness, and being on the same page with team mates. When working with others towards a common goal, it is important to convey ideas clearly and effectively to foster mutual understanding.

Passion

I believe that passion is important because it is motivation and drive. To me, passion comes from feeling that your work is meaningful and has a real, positive impact on people. Feeling like my work is meaningful and real helps me feel excited, work hard toward my goals, and persevere through obstacles.
